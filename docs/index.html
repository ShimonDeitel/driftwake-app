The problem

Clock-time alarms ignore how long it took you to fall asleep

Set an alarm for "8 hours from now" and it fires 8 hours from when you pressed the button — not from when you actually drifted off. Take 40 minutes to fall asleep, and every night is a small, compounding mismatch.

On-device detection

A real heuristic, not a guess

No cloud, no wearable, no machine-learning model to trust blindly — just your phone's motion sensor and microphone, watched for a sustained drop in variance.

⟳

Motion stillness

Driftwake reads accelerometer variance minute by minute. About 8 quiet minutes in a row is the signature of having actually drifted off — not a single still moment, a sustained one.

◔

Room-sound confirmation

The microphone's metered loudness (never recorded, never saved) backs up the motion signal — a quiet, still room, minute after minute.

◑

Back-dated onset

Once confirmed, onset is placed at the start of that still run, not the moment it was confirmed — so your anchor counts from when sleep actually began.

The quirky part

A second-chance snooze that's actually honest

Hit snooze and Driftwake checks your motion/mic signature during that window — instead of always granting it, or never.

Light sleep

Snooze granted. Low but nonzero motion — you're still drifting, not flatlined. Nine more minutes, no questions asked.

Deep sleep

Snooze locked out. Sustained near-zero variance for minutes on end reads as deep sleep — the button disables itself, with a plain explanation of why.

Nightly AI insight — Pro

It learns what actually leaves you rested

Log a 1–5 grogginess rating each morning. Driftwake looks at your last 14 nights of anchor duration versus how you felt, and hands back one plain sentence — like "you feel sharper at 5.5 hours than 6 — try that tonight."
