A clock alarm ignores how long you actually slept. Set it for 6:40, take 40 minutes to fall asleep, and the eight hours you planned quietly become seven and change. Driftwake works the other way around: it starts counting from the moment sleep begins, so eight hours means eight real hours.

Choose an anchor duration, plain hours or whole 90-minute sleep cycles. On the main screen, a small ember flickers while Driftwake waits for sleep onset, then settles into a steady glow the instant onset locks, and a circular countdown ring begins tracing toward your wake point. When the alarm fires, the second-chance snooze checks the recent motion and sound stillness from your nightstand: if the readings look like deep sleep, snooze is disabled for the night with a plain explanation, otherwise you get one more round. Each morning you can log a 1 to 5 grogginess rating for that night's duration.

Free includes one saved anchor profile with manual anchoring: tap "I'm about to sleep" and that tap starts the count. Driftwake Pro, an auto-renewing monthly subscription at $4.99 per month, adds automatic onset detection using on-device motion and microphone stillness (only the sound level is measured in real time, no audio is ever recorded or uploaded), multiple saved anchor profiles for weeknights, naps, or jet lag, and a morning AI insight. The insight sends your last 14 days of duration and grogginess pairs to be processed via our servers, no account needed, and replies with one plain sentence suggesting a specific duration to try. No history is kept server-side, only the on-device log.

Automatic detection keeps sampling while your phone rests on the nightstand. If iOS suspends the app overnight, the manual tap remains the reliable fallback, and Driftwake tells you so up front. Wake up measured from when sleep actually began.
